Lazeez – Visual Storytelling for Dining

UI/UX Designer & Frontend Developer | HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript, GSAP

💐 Project Overview

Lazeez is a modern, fully responsive restaurant website that I designed and developed from scratch. The goal? Create a smooth, visually rich user experience that feels like stepping into a stylish dining space — all through the browser.

This wasn’t just about making things look pretty — I wanted the site to tell a story, highlight the restaurant’s personality, and make it effortless for users to explore the menu or book a table.

🎯 Objective

My Mission was to:

  • Build a clean, responsive layout that works on all devices
  • Use animation to guide users and create a sense of flow
  • Keep navigation intuitive and lightweight
  • Show off the menu in a way that’s both aesthetic and functional
  • Develop everything with performance and accessibility in mind
🛠️ Tools & Stack
  • HTML/CSS:For structure and a custom, elegant visual style
  • Bootstrap:For responsive layout and grid handling
  • JavaScript:For interactivity and functional components (like nav and form behavior)
  • GSAP:To animate scrolls, fades, and transitions — adding life to every section
  • Netlify:For fast deployment and hosting
🧠 The Process
  • Inspiration & Planning

I started by researching restaurant websites and picking out what worked — and what didn’t. I noticed that many had either a great visual identity or great UX — but rarely both. I set out to combine those two.

  • Wireframing & Layout

Mapped out the site structure: Hero, About, Menu, Booking, Contact. I planned a scroll-based layout where each section would flow naturally into the next — guided by soft animations and strategic content placement.

  • Design

Used spacious layout, soft glows, and smooth fonts to maintain a balance between tech and wonder. Visual hierarchy and contrast were refined for readability and engagement.

  • Chose a rich color palette with high contrast to evoke appetite and warmth
  • Used custom fonts from Google Fonts for a mix of modern and classy
  • Focused heavily on spacing and typography to keep things readable and uncluttered
    • Development Phase
  • Wrote clean, semantic HTML with reusable Bootstrap grid layouts
  • Added subtle GSAP animations to enhance transitions and user engagement
  • Focused on smooth scroll behavior, consistent styles, and responsive design
  • Implemented a simple reservation form and built out navigation behavior with JavaScript
    • ✨ Key Features
    • Fully responsive design
    • Animated scroll-triggered sections (GSAP)
    • Eye-catching food menu with hover effects
    • Clean, fixed navbar with smooth scroll
    • Lightweight form for reservations
    • Fast load time and mobile-first optimizations
    🚧 Challenges
    • Tuning GSAP animations to be subtle but noticeable
    • Getting consistent padding/margins across all screen sizes
    • Designing a layout that’s content-rich but never overwhelming
    • Balancing elegance with performance and accessibility
    💡 What I Learned
    • How powerful scroll-based storytelling can be for a restaurant brand
    • The impact of micro-interactions on user engagement
    • Practical use of GSAP and Bootstrap together
    • How to approach a full solo project — from design thinking to production-ready code
    💖 What Made It Special

    Lazeez was one of those projects where I got to wear every hat — and loved every second of it. It’s a blend of creativity, structure, and subtle animation — and for me, it marked a big step in leveling up both my design eye and frontend skills.

    🏁 Why It Matters

    This wasn’t just another portfolio project — Lazeez was my way of blending code with creativity. It taught me how to craft a digital experience that feels personal, immersive, and easy to navigate. More than that, it showed me the power of great UI to make even something as everyday as a restaurant visit feel special online.

    🚀 Final Thoughts

    Building Lazeez from scratch pushed me to think deeply about flow, emotion, and interaction. From the animations to the spacing, every detail was intentional. It’s a project I’m truly proud of — and a strong reflection of where I’m headed as a frontend developer and UI/UX designer.
